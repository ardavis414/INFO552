Reference: Descriptive Cataloging 101 LibGuide

The following LibGuide was researched and created as a term final for a users, services, and resources course. The purpose of this LibGuide is to support the training of novice catalogers who currently volunteer in Philadelphia's public school libraries. Without professional librarians on staff in elementary schools, they rely on retired volunteers to keep their libraries running.

Metadata and Resource Description: MODS Repositories Analysis and Assessment

Abstract: Despite the development and widespread adoption of metadata schemas and standards, evidence suggests that libraries, archives, and museums still experience issues with search-ability and interoperability within their metadata records. This report reviews 60 total MODS-XML records from three institutions: Columbia University Libraries, JISC, and Brown University Library to evaluate each collection's average rates of accuracy and completion in accordance to the MODS implementation guidelines and Library of Congress Authorities schemas. The selected data was used as evidence to support the hypothesis that inconsistent and/or inaccurate implementation of MODS elements in database records causes decreased interoperability amongst the three repositories. Additionally, inaccurate mapping between display terms and MODS elements hinders users' chances of completing successful targeted and exploratory database inquiries.
